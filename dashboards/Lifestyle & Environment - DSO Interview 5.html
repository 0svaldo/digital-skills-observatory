Where do you spend most of your time?

About 70% of the respondents say they spend most time at work. 'At home' and 'Market' are a distant second.

Men more often answer 'Work' to this question, and women have a larger 'At home' segment.

Statements on food, money, and living standards

When asked to choose between a few statements on food and money, we see differences between the groups, but it's a mixed bag. In the treatment group, the percentage of people that have enough food and money for their everyday needs is a bit higher (but still at a paltry 14%), but the percentage of people that sometimes skip a meal or are really struggeling is higher. There weren't any large differences between genders for this question.

When we look at statements on living standards, we see a small gender difference: females are more likely to say they live comfortably than men, and men are a bit more likely to say they always live comfortably. There was no discernible group difference for this question.

What do you think about learning new digital skills?

This is a great general question about how the respondents feel about learning new digital skills. Men are a bit more likely than women to say that it's easy for them, and the treatment group also shows 10 percent more respondents saying that it's easy for them to learn new digital skills.

The amount of respondents saying that it's very difficult for them is small, but is noticeably higher for the control group.
