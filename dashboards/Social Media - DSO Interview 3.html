Use of Social Media

Insert paragraph here...

Treatment vs Control: For the first section below we look at some answers to Social Media related questions, group by group, comparing the respondents from the treatment group with those from the control group. 

Both groups have a sizeable minority of respondents that report they do not use multiple digital accounts: 26 in the treatment group and 27 in the control group. However, there seems to be a high level of uncertainty what the word "account" means among all respondents (over half of all respondents did not seem to know the meaning of the word). When comparing results from both questions (Q1: Do you use multiple digital account? and Q3 Does the respondent understand the word account?) the two are highly correlated: 75% of the people that said they did not use multiple digital accounts, also did not understand the word "account". This makes it possible that some of these respondents actually do have digital accounts to log in and use social media and other apps but did not understand question 1.

Women are more likely to use multiple accounts than men.

Comparing all respondents: The following section presents data on all 177 respondents. Every chart contains a filter which lets you select the data of the Treatment or Control group separately.

An impressive number of respondents (145 of the 177) said they had opened a new social media account since the last interview. There's no discernible difference between the treatment and the control group on this indicator though: both groups have an equal proportion of Yes and No answers to question 'Social Media Q7'. 

Equally, a vast majority of the respondents (163 out of 177) have used social media since the last interview. And again, the proportions within the treatment and control groups show no difference.  Messenger apps and Facebook top the list of accounts mentioned; they were used by 141 and 140 respondents respectively. Instagram (used by 45 respondents) and Gmail (used by 32 respondents) trail this top 2.

A considerable amount of respondents has also stopped using some social media accounts since the last interview. Again, Messenger apps and Facebook feature the top of the list,  albeit only being mentioned 10 and 8 times respectively.

Understanding of Social Media


For demo purposes we coded the responses to the question "Does the respondent understand the word "account"?" Some of the original answers noted down were not immediately clear for interpretation and were marked "Unclear".

Even with 20% of the responses marked "Unclear", a majority (at least 53.1%) of the respondents does not properly understand the word "account".

Similarly, a majority of respondents (54%) indicated there are some icons or symbols they did not understand. On this indicator, the treatment group and control group differ significantly: the treatment group has a larger proportion of respondents signalling an issue with icons and symbols than the control group (59% vs 49%).

The (Google) Drive icon was mentioned separately by most respondents (22 times) as something they did not understand, but most icons or symbols were only mentioned by a handful of respondents. There is a wide variety of over 50 different icons and symbols mentioned by the respondents.

Willingness to Learn


An important section to compare the treatment and control groups on is the inclination to learn more about social media. The answers to the question "Is there a specific social media that want to learn and install?" are presented per group below. While about two thirds of the treatment group (60 out of 95) answered this question with Yes, the control group was evenly split on this issue: only 51% answered Yes (42 out of 82).

Instagram and Twitter are the social media platforms mentioned most often by the respondents but there is a wide variety of 17 different products/platforms mentioned, and most are only mentioned by a handful of respondents.
